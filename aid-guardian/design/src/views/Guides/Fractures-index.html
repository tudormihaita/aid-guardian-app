Fractures First Aid Instructions

Fractures, or broken bones, require immediate medical attention. Here are the steps you should follow:

Steps to Treat Fractures:

  1. Stop Any Bleeding: If the person is bleeding, apply pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
  2. Immobilize the Injured Area: Keep the injured area as still as possible. Use a splint or sling to immobilize the limb.
  3. Apply Cold: Use an ice pack or cold compress to reduce swelling and pain. Do not apply ice directly to the skin.
  4. Provide Pain Relief: Give over-the-counter pain relievers like ibuprofen or acetaminophen if the person is in pain.
  5. Seek Medical Attention: Call emergency services or take the person to the nearest hospital or urgent care center.

For Open Fractures:

  1. Do Not Push the Bone Back In: Do not attempt to push a bone back in if it is protruding through the skin.
  2. Cover the Wound: Cover the wound with a sterile bandage or clean cloth to prevent infection.

For Suspected Neck or Back Injuries:

  1. Do Not Move the Person: Do not move the person unless absolutely necessary, such as if they are in immediate danger.
  2. Keep the Person Still: Encourage the person to remain as still as possible. Use rolled towels or cushions to keep the neck and head stable.
  3. Call Emergency Services: Seek immediate medical help.

Special Considerations:

Always suspect a fracture if there is severe pain, swelling, bruising, or an inability to move the limb. In the case of a suspected fracture, it is better to be safe and seek medical attention even if you are not sure.
