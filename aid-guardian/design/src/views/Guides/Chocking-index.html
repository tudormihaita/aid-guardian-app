Choking First Aid Instructions

If someone is choking, they need immediate help. Here are the steps you should follow:

For an Adult or Child over One Year Old:

If the person can cough forcefully, encourage them to keep coughing.

If the person cannot cough, speak, or breathe, use the Heimlich maneuver:

  1. Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.
  2. Make a fist with one hand. Place the thumb side of your fist just above the person's navel, below the ribcage.
  3. Grasp your fist with your other hand and press into the person’s abdomen with a quick, upward thrust.
  4. Repeat thrusts until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.

If the Person Becomes Unconscious:

Lower them to the ground and start CPR:

  1. Kneel next to the person's shoulders.
  2. Begin chest compressions by placing the heel of one hand on the center of the chest and your other hand on top. Push hard and fast, compressing at least 2 inches deep at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.
  3. After 30 compressions, check the mouth for any visible obstructions. Remove any object you can see and reach.
  4. Attempt to give two rescue breaths. Tilt the head back, lift the chin, and give a breath to make the chest rise. If the chest doesn’t rise, reposition and try again.
  5. Continue with cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives or the person starts to breathe normally.

For an Infant Under One Year Old:

If the infant is coughing or gagging, let them try to clear the object.

If the infant cannot cough, cry, or breathe, do the following:

  1. Sit down and hold the infant face down on your forearm, resting on your thigh. Support the infant's head and jaw with your hand.
  2. Give up to 5 back blows between the infant’s shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.
  3. If the object is not expelled, turn the infant face up, resting on your thigh, and give up to 5 chest thrusts. Place two fingers in the center of the infant’s chest just below the nipple line, and press down about 1.5 inches.
  4. Repeat the sequence of 5 back blows and 5 chest thrusts until the object is expelled or the infant becomes unresponsive.

If the Infant Becomes Unresponsive:

Begin infant CPR:

  1. Place the infant on a flat surface and start chest compressions by using two fingers to press down in the center of the chest just below the nipple line, compressing about 1.5 inches deep.
  2. Give 30 compressions at a rate of 100-120 per minute, followed by two rescue breaths.
  3. Continue with cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives or the infant starts to breathe normally.
